1 Summary

In this article, phone-based accelerometers are used to attain activity recognition. Kwapisz, Weiss & Moore [1] categorized accelerometer data from twenty-nine users, while users are walking, jogging, climbing stairs, sitting and standing, because these activities are performed regularly. With the accumulated data, they would then create a model suited for activity recognition. Activity recognition will help in myriad areas. For example, redirecting calls to voicemail while the users perform certain exercises, generate profile activity on a daily/weekly basis, which helps determine health conditions, and provide significant reports such as calories consumption. Subsequently, an appropriate action can be taken to assist the user to improve accordingly, for instance alerting the user if they consumed more calories than advised. They used a permitted user interface to collect and pass the data to their WISDM project [18] for quality check. 20 samples per second was sufficient for the supervised learning task. Then a set of features is generated each containing 200 raw accelerometer readings, and each reading has an x, y and z value for each dimension. Horizontal, upward and downward motion, and forward movement, respectively. Subsequently their results showed distinctive patterns on the axis of standing and sitting and periodic behavior among the other activities. Moreover they achieved above 90% accuracy for walking and jogging. In addition, and after the confusion resulted from climbing stairs movements, they combined them into one activity. And the Multilayer Perception algorithm performed the best; overall accuracy of 91.7%. Although similar works have been done [2-17], their approach [1] differs in the usage of devices, which are mass-marketed instead of research-only, one device usage, which was put in the pocket of the users, and the fact that most previous studies conducted research with less users than theirs; i.e. twenty-nine. Moreover, they aim to provide and publish the collected data and believe that with the help of their work, many opportunities will be taken for mining wireless sensor data.
